--- layout: home title: DYNOMO subtitle: Dynamic Observation Modeling of digital constructions --- 3D Construction Models

3D digital construction models are a cornerstone of digitally twinning built environments as they define the components within the structure, representation, and functionality to reflect real-world behavior in a digital environment. Conventional design models restrict the representation of components, which ensures proper functionality but has issues with reality approximation and requires immense human effort for their creation. Alternatively, sensory observations (i.e., close-range point clouds, imagery) provide a much more detailed representation of the construction but lack functionality and structure. This limitation is due to the lack of modeling mechanisms, not the quality of the used observations. Evidence exists that such functionality and structure can be directly derived from observations through neural networks without human intervention. This concept of 'observation modeling' has immense potential but does not currently exist in the construction domain.

  1. Component representations are formulated as Implicit Functions, which allows functionality (e.g., object changes or finite element discretization) to be expressed as dynamic solutions rather than through heuristics.

DYNOMO will overcome the main challenges of inverse design of 3D construction models from observations, thereby bringing observation models to the same level as design models but without the extremely cumbersome and abstract design process. This knowledge will lead to unprecedented model realism that has a major impact on applications ranging from structural analysis and maintenance to simulations and design.
